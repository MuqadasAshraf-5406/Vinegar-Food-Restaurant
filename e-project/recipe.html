Recipe of the month:

Prawn karahi


A Prawn Karahi curry is a seafood spin on the more classic and common chicken, lamb or mutton Karahi. Just like its aforementioned counterparts, it’s essentially a prawn curry cooked in a deliciously thick tomato, ginger and garlic gravy (masala). As per tradition, it’s usually served garnished with coriander, green chilli and julienne-cut slices of ginger.


Instructions:

Heat the oil in a pan. Once the oil is hot, flash fry the prawns for one minute. Set aside In the same oil, add in the minced ginger and garlic and fry for 2 minutes Add all the spices and green chillis.Add the tomatoes. Cook this on high heat, stirring. Allow this to cook for 5-10 minutes, until the gravy is thick and you can see oil separating around the edge of the pan. You'll also see a lot of holes bubbling in the gravy. Once the gravy is ready, add back the prawns and continue to cook this on high for a further 5 min. Your prawn karahi is ready! Garnish with freshly chopped coriander, matchstick-cut ginger, kalonji seeds and green chillis.

Ingredients:

    1- Oil or ghee
    2- Garlic
    3- Ginger
    4- Tomatoes pureed
    5- Raw prawns
    6- Green chillis
    7- Salt
    8- Paprika
    9- Turmeric
    10- Cumin powder
    11- Coriander powder
    12- Kalonji (black seeds)
    13- Sugar
    14- Red chilli powder
